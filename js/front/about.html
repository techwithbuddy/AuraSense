About AuraSense

AuraSense is dedicated to making the web and digital resources accessible for people with visual impairments. Below you'll find our mission, features, accessibility specifications, and privacy notes.

Our mission

We provide practical guides, training, assistive-technology recommendations, and community support so people with vision loss can access information and participate fully online. We focus on usability, low-barrier entry, and evidence-based resources.

Assistive demo

  • Step-by-step tutorials for screen readers, magnifiers, and accessible mobile features
  • Reviews and recommendations for assistive hardware and software
  • Community support groups, local meetups, and volunteer programs
  • Workshops and training geared toward digital accessibility

Accessibility & technical specifications

We design the site to be inclusive and resilient. Key specifications include:

  • Semantic HTML and ARIA where needed for assistive technologies
  • High contrast and adjustable font sizes (keyboard controls available on the homepage)
  • Keyboard-first navigation â€” all interactive elements are focusable and usable without a mouse
  • Responsive design for mobile and desktop (WCAG-friendly breakpoints)
  • Polyglot-friendly markup and progressive enhancement for assistive devices

Authentication and the logo

The logo in the header includes a lock badge to convey the authentication and secure aspects of our services. Clicking the logo (from other pages) goes to this page so visitors can learn more about our aims, data handling, and specifications.

We do not collect more personal data than necessary for participation. For any authentication features we use standard, secure practices and open standards; details are available on request.

How to use this site

Use the keyboard shortcuts shown on the homepage (Alt + R to read, Alt + + / - to resize text). Reach out via the contact form on the home page for help or to request specific accessibility accommodations.

Contact & feedback

If you have questions, accessibility feedback, or want to contribute resources, email us at hello@aurasense.org.
