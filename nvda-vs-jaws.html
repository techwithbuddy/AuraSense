NVDA vs JAWS

A concise comparison to help you choose a desktop screen reader.

Criteria NVDA JAWS
PriceFree, open-sourcePaid (licence required)
PlatformWindowsWindows
Learning curveModerate; good community guidesEasier for beginners with vendor support
StrengthsExtensible, community-drivenCommercial support, polished features
Who it's forUsers who want free, powerful toolsUsers who need vendor support or advanced features

Recommendation

If budget is important, start with NVDA; if you need commercial support and advanced integrations, evaluate JAWS (trial available).
