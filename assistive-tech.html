Assistive Apps

Screen readers (NVDA, JAWS, VoiceOver, TalkBack)

What they do: Read the screen aloud and expose UI elements to keyboard users. Best for: Blind users who need full desktop or mobile access.

Formats: Free (NVDA, TalkBack, VoiceOver built-in), Paid (JAWS).

OCR apps (Seeing AI, Envision AI)

What they do: Use the camera to recognize text, objects, and describe scenes. Best for: Low-vision users and blind people who need on-demand recognition.

Price: Free / Freemium depending on platform.

Navigation apps

What they do: Provide turn-by-turn directions and points-of-interest specifically for blind users (e.g., Soundscape, BlindSquare).

Voice-to-text apps

What they do: Convert speech to text for messaging and notes (e.g., Google Live Transcribe, built-in dictation).

Hardware recommendations

Braille displays

What they do: Provide tactile reading via refreshable braille. Best for: Braille-literate users who prefer tactile output.

Talking watches & wearables

What they do: Provide time, alerts, and simple notifications by voice or haptics. Best for: users needing quick context without opening a phone.

Smart canes & navigation hardware

What they do: Offer obstacle detection and orientation assistance. Best for: independent mobility users in urban environments.

Headphones for navigation

What they do: Deliver clear directional audio and spoken guidance; look for open-ear designs for environmental awareness.
